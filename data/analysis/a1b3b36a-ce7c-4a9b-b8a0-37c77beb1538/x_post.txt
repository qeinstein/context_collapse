I ran a controlled long-context experiment on an LLM.

Result:
- baseline accuracy: 98.8%
- random ~4000-word noise: 97.5%
- semantically similar ~1000-word noise: 82.5%

Key finding: the model didn’t fail because the prompt was long.
It failed because semantically similar distractors caused retrieval interference.

For retrieval tasks, distractor adoption under similar_1000 was 24.0%.
Wrong answers were copied from irrelevant context.
